# Product

## Register

product

## Users

PyMEs (small and medium businesses) and entrepreneurs in Mexico. They are the business owner or administrator, logging in from the office or on-the-go to issue electronic invoices (CFDI), manage customers, and track billing. They use this daily, during active billing moments — focused, not browsing. They expect the tool to get out of the way and let them work.

## Product Purpose

Kipo is a CFDI / electronic invoicing platform for Mexican SMBs. It handles invoice issuance (timbrado), customer management, billing history, and stamp (sello) packs. Success means a business owner can open Kipo, find what they need, and get back to running their business in under 60 seconds.

## Brand Personality

Confiable, ágil, mexicano. The brand feels confident and professional without being cold — it's a tool built specifically for Mexican businesses, not a US SaaS product translated to Spanish. The red (#e00b26) is energetic and decisive; the lime accent is unexpected and modern.

## Anti-references

- Generic US SaaS with cream/neutral palette translated to Spanish (SAP, Facturelo-style interfaces)
- Heavy corporate accounting software (Contpaq, CONTPAQi visual language)
- Over-simplified "startup light" tools that don't feel serious enough for tax compliance work

## Design Principles

1. **Speed over ceremony.** Users are in a billing flow. Every extra tap or confusing label costs real time. Default to the most direct path.
2. **Mexican context, not translated US product.** Copy, flows, and patterns should feel native to how Mexican SMBs operate — including WhatsApp OTP as a first-class auth option.
3. **Trust through clarity.** Invoice/tax tools need to feel reliable. Clear hierarchy, predictable components, no visual surprises. Familiarity is a feature.
4. **Brand at entry, restraint inside.** Auth screens can carry the brand boldly; the working dashboard steps back and lets the data speak.
5. **Every state accounted for.** Empty, loading, error, success — no partial states ship. Users filing invoices can't encounter a UI that gives them no feedback.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. All form controls keyboard-navigable. OTP flow must work without a smartphone camera (manual code entry, not QR). Spanish-language first.
